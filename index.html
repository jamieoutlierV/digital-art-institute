The art of now

Every field has a moment where the practice outpaces the language. The emergent digital arts are in that moment. The work is already happening. It’s plural, restless, globally distributed. What’s missing is the infrastructure to make sense of it collectively.

A(DAI) starts with a conversation. Deep listening. What practitioners say before it turns into theory or doctrine. From there it senses, finding the patterns that cut across scenes, subcultures, and geographies. It queries. It speculates. It reacts. From that process, a shared language emerges. A collective memory. Something you can build on, fork, query, discover through.

It’s a place to find collaborators. To see how your practice connects to others you didn’t know existed. To contribute to a narrative that belongs to the field, not to an institution.

A(DAI) is an open protocol. Anyone can build on top of it.

Narrative formation led by artists first. Market formation follows.
